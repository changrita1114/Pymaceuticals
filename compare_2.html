Results in Figures

Figure 3.
Boxplots for 5 regimens which had less total tumor volumes at last measurement points. There was a significant difference in total tumor volumes between 5 regimens (p < 0.05, ANOVA). However, there was no significant difference in total tumor volumes between Ramicane and Capomulin (p=0.77, unpaired-t test).
Figure 4.
Regression and correlation analysis in Ramicane. The regression line indicates that the higher the body weight, the higher the total tumor volume. There was a strong correlation between body weight and tumor volumes (r=0.81, pearson correlation coefficient).
Figure 5.
Regression and correlation analysis in Capomulin. The regression line indicates that the higher the body weight, the higher the total tumor volume. There was a strong correlation between body weight and tumor volumes (r=0.84, pearson correlation coefficient).
Figure 6.
Regression and correlation analysis in Propriva. There was no correlation between body weight and tumor volumes (r=0.1, pearson correlation coefficient).
Figure 7.
Regression and correlation analysis in Ceftamin. There was no correlation between body weight and tumor volumes (r=0.04, pearson correlation coefficient).
Figure 8.
Regression and correlation analysis in Inbubinol. There was no correlation between body weight and tumor volumes (r=0.06, pearson correlation coefficient).
